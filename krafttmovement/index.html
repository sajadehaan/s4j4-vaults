Module 01

Who you are

You are someone who looked at what everyone around you was doing and decided it wasn't enough. Not as a statement — just as a fact. Pilates was beautiful but incomplete. Gyms worked the body but not the understanding. You wanted something that "actually works" — and when Berlin didn't have it, you built it. You run a studio in Prenzlauer Berg where people walk in curious and leave changed. Not because you promise transformation, but because you put them in a room at 45 degrees and wait. "Something cracked open. That moment. When the body overtakes the mind." That's what you're after. Every week. That's the only metric you actually care about.

Module 02

Why you do what you do

WHY

Why

Because beautiful movement without strength is incomplete — and strength without understanding the body is just noise. "I wanted to create a place where people actually work, not pose." Berlin's directness matched that conviction. This city doesn't tolerate bullshit. Neither does KRAFTT.

HOW

How

By refusing to choose between rehabilitative and athletic. The KRAFTT Method layers Pilates alignment with serious strength training, then adds heat as a deliberate physiological tool — not a gimmick, not a brand decision. "Alignment and strength reinforce each other — you get stronger by understanding what moved you, not just how hard." The 45-degree environment is the proof of concept.

WHAT

What

A weekly ritual at Greifswalder Str. 223, Berlin-Prenzlauer Berg: Pilates-based strength training in 45-degree heat. One method. Serious results. No frills.

Module 03

Who you attract — and who you don't

Who it's for

They're 28–40, living a full life in Berlin, and tired of wellness that asks nothing of them. They don't Google studios for weeks — they try something once and know immediately. They want one hour that counts. Not a reset, not a detox — proof. The kind that shows up the next morning in how they carry themselves. They care about their bodies without making it their whole personality.

Who it's not for

This is not for someone looking for a gentle introduction to movement or a low-intensity sweat. It's not for people who want weight-loss framing or a class that adjusts itself to avoid discomfort. If you need soft Pilates to feel safe, KRAFTT will frustrate you. And that's honest. Some people need to build a base elsewhere first — that's not failure, it's sequencing.

Module 07

How you summarise yourself

01

Proof lives in the body.

KRAFTT Movement's entire philosophy — "Not theory. Proof." — collapsed into five words that land as a statement and a dare simultaneously.

02

Heat is not the point. Almost.

Disarms the obvious question about the 45-degree format with honesty and wit — the kind of voice that signals this studio won't waste your time.

03

Strength built from understanding.

Positions KRAFTT's differentiation without naming competitors — implies all the studios that skip the "why" of the body in favour of just pushing harder.

04

One hour that actually counts.

Speaks directly to KRAFTT Movement's target client — the busy Berlin 28–40 who wants return on time, not a lifestyle product.

05

Sweat. Strengthen. Transform.

Literally from KRAFTT Movement's own words. Three imperatives that build in escalating intensity — sweat is the entry point, transform is the earned outcome, and the rhythm is clean enough to live on a wall, a shirt, or a booking page.

Recommended

S4J4 Recommendation

Option 05 is the starting point: KRAFTT Movement's own three-word sequence has the most load because it builds from the physical (sweat) through the functional (strengthen) to the ultimate outcome (transform) in the exact order the class delivers it. Use it unpolished or replace "Transform" with something more concrete — "Transform" is the one word here that risks abstraction. Consider: "Sweat. Strengthen. Know." or keep the original and let the class itself prove the third word true.

Short Bio

About page · sidebar · social bio

KRAFTT Movement is a studio in Berlin-Prenzlauer Berg built on one refusal: choosing between Pilates and real strength training. The KRAFTT Method runs both at 45 degrees — not as a concept, but as a weekly practice. The studio opened because the city needed somewhere that takes bodies seriously without needing to make it a lifestyle.

Long Bio

Full About page · Tier 3 website

I started as a Pilates instructor. The work was precise and I believed in it — but I kept feeling like something was missing. People were getting mobile. They weren't getting strong. And nobody was asking whether those two things could happen in the same room.

So I started building toward that. I read the sports science. I trained myself differently. I looked at what heat actually does to muscle tissue when the conditions are right — not as a wellness trend, but as a physiological fact. And I tested it.

The KRAFTT Method came out of that process. Pilates alignment as the foundation. Serious strength work built on top of it. The 45-degree environment as a tool — because the heat changes what the body is willing to do, and when something shifts in that room, you feel it.

Berlin made sense for this. The city doesn't reward bullshit. People here want to know: does this work? They want proof.

That's what I'm offering. One class. Come and find out.
